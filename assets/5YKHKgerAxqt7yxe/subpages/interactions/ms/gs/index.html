Goal - Setting a SMART Goal

The first step in the GROW model is to clearly define the goal or outcome you want to achieve. We'll help you set a SMART goal by guiding you through each component: Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, and Time-bound.

Process: Answer the following questions to articulate your SMART goal.

Example: I want to improve my math grades.
Example: By increasing my math test scores from 75% to 85%.
Example: Yes, by studying an extra hour each day and attending online tutoring sessions.
Example: Improving my math grade is essential for my college application and future career in engineering.
Example: By the end of the semester, in three months.
Example: I will improve my math test scores from 75% to 85% by studying an extra hour each day and attending online tutoring sessions, achieving this by the end of the semester in three months to strengthen my college application.

Reality - Where are you now?

The second step is to understand the current reality and gather information about your situation right now.

Process: Reflect on your current circumstances. Be honest in your assessment without judging yourself.

Example: I complete my homework but don't spend extra time reviewing the material.
Example: Distractions at home and lack of motivation are slowing me down.
Example: Studying alone without a clear plan isn't improving my understanding.
Example: I've tried studying with friends, but we often get off-topic.

Options - What could you do to accomplish your goal?

The third step is to explore potential options and strategies to achieve the goal.

Process: Brainstorm various ideas and alternatives.

Example: Attend online tutoring, join a study group, or use educational apps.
Example: Ask my teacher for extra help or find additional resources online.
Example: They set specific study times and use practice tests to improve.

Way Forward - What will you do?

The last step is to establish a clear plan of action and commit to moving forward.

Process: Identify specific steps, priorities, and a timeline for implementing your chosen options.

Example: Attending online tutoring sessions and setting a daily study schedule.
Example: I will sign up for tutoring tonight and start my study schedule tomorrow.
Example: I'll track my progress in a journal and check in weekly with my teacher.
Example: By removing distractions during study time and staying motivated by remembering my goals.
Example: I'll ask my parents to check my study schedule and encourage me.
